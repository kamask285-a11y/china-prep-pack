# User Personas

## Core target user
The MVP is designed for first-time travelers to China who:

- use English
- have little or no Chinese ability
- prefer free or semi-independent travel
- are comfortable using a smartphone
- feel some anxiety around logistics, communication, or payment

The goal is not to serve every international traveler at first. The goal is to serve a narrow, high-value segment well.

---

## Persona 1: Jack, the solo explorer

### Profile
- Age: 29
- Country: United States
- Travel style: solo, flexible, experience-driven
- Trip length: 10 days
- Cities: Beijing, Shanghai, Chengdu

### Traits
- first time in China
- fluent in English
- no Chinese ability
- comfortable with apps and digital tools
- likes freedom, but wants a smooth arrival

### Main concerns
- Will payment work?
- How do I get to my hotel after landing?
- What if I cannot communicate clearly?
- What are the minimum things I need ready before the trip?

### What he wants
- a short list of what matters most
- clear arrival-day steps
- a driver card with Chinese address
- backup plans if something fails

### Why he would use the product
Jack does not want to read ten blog posts. He wants one practical pack that helps him arrive prepared.

## Persona 2: Emma and Ryan, the comfort-focused couple

### Profile
- Age: 30 and 32
- Country: United Kingdom
- Travel style: couple travel, semi-planned, comfort-first
- Trip length: 7 days
- Cities: Shanghai, Hangzhou

### Traits
- first time in China
- English-speaking
- moderate research behavior before trips
- do not want heavy friction or last-minute confusion

### Main concerns
- What do we absolutely need to prepare?
- What can be ignored for now?
- How do we reduce stress on day one?
- How do we avoid getting stuck in small situations?

### What they want
- a priority-based checklist
- a first-day guide with simple decisions
- confidence that they are not missing anything important

### Why they would use the product
They value convenience and clarity more than depth. A structured prep pack saves them time and stress.

---

## Persona 3: Aisha, the restriction-sensitive traveler

### Profile
- Age: 31
- Country: Malaysia
- Travel style: organized, cautious, comfort-aware
- Trip length: 6 days
- Cities: Shanghai, Beijing

### Traits
- first time in China
- English-speaking
- Muslim traveler
- highly sensitive to food and communication accuracy

### Main concerns
- How do I explain food restrictions clearly?
- How do I reduce the chance of misunderstanding in restaurants?
- What should I prepare before departure to feel safe and comfortable?

### What she wants
- food restriction communication cards
- better prioritization around dining and communication
- lower-risk first-meal guidance
- clear reminders about what matters most for her profile

### Why she would use the product
Generic guides do not reflect her needs well. She values preparation that adapts to dietary and religious requirements.

---

## Persona 4: Lucas, the low-tech anxious traveler

### Profile
- Age: 42
- Country: France
- Travel style: independent but cautious
- Trip length: 8 days
- Cities: Shanghai, Chongqing

### Traits
- first time in China
- limited Chinese
- lower comfort with digital travel setup
- gets stressed by too many apps and unclear steps

### Main concerns
- Which apps do I really need?
- What should I do first, second, and third?
- What happens if internet or payment does not work?

### What he wants
- fewer but more important steps
- simple explanations
- visible backup plans
- reduced cognitive overload

### Why he would use the product
He does not want a complex travel tool. He wants a calm, minimal setup assistant.

## Persona summary matrix

| Persona | Key constraint | Key value needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jack | no Chinese, solo, multi-city | transport, payment, confidence |
| Emma & Ryan | comfort-first, limited prep time | prioritization, simplicity |
| Aisha | food and religion constraints | accurate communication, safe meal planning |
| Lucas | low tech confidence | minimal steps, fallback plans |

---

## Product implication
The personas show that the product must not behave like a generic travel guide.

Different users should receive meaningfully different outputs.

Examples:
- users with no Chinese ability should see communication tools more prominently
- users with dietary restrictions should see food communication earlier
- solo travelers should see stronger backup and emergency guidance
- low-tech users should get fewer recommended tools and clearer sequencing
