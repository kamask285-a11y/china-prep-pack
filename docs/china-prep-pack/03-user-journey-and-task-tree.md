# User Journey and Task Tree

## Purpose of this document
This document maps the pre-departure and arrival journey of a first-time traveler to China.

Its job is to answer two questions:

1. What is the traveler trying to do at each stage?
2. Where can China Prep Pack create the most value in the MVP?

The focus of the MVP is not the entire trip. The focus is the high-anxiety period before departure and the first day after arrival.

---

# 1. Journey stages

## Stage 1: 7-14 days before departure

### User goal
Understand what needs to be prepared before leaving.

### User behavior
- searches online for China travel tips
- watches videos or reads posts
- compares what different people recommend
- starts worrying about things like payment, apps, internet, and language

### User pain points
- too much conflicting information
- cannot tell what is essential
- generic advice does not fit personal needs
- unclear sequence of actions

### Product opportunity
Provide a clear preparation framework and a personalized checklist.

---

## Stage 2: 1-3 days before departure

### User goal
Make sure all important travel basics are actually ready.

### User behavior
- checks documents and bookings
- installs apps
- saves addresses and confirmations
- tries to think through arrival day

### User pain points
- forgets small but critical details
- does not prepare fallback plans
- feels anxiety because too many things remain vague

### Product opportunity
Turn abstract planning into a concrete action pack with priorities and fallback options.

---

## Stage 3: Flight and pre-landing moment

### User goal
Be mentally ready for the first few hours after arrival.

### User behavior
- reviews bookings
- thinks about airport transfer
- worries about payment, internet, and communication

### User pain points
- uncertainty about what to do first
- arrival anxiety
- no clear first-step sequence

### Product opportunity
Provide a short arrival sequence with only the most important steps.

---

## Stage 4: Airport arrival and first transfer

### User goal
Get from airport to hotel smoothly.

### User behavior
- turns on phone
- looks for internet access
- opens hotel information
- chooses a transport option
- tries to communicate destination

### User pain points
- internet may not be ready
- payment may feel uncertain
- language barrier becomes real
- too many decisions at once

### Product opportunity
Provide address visibility, transport planning, backup payment awareness, and communication cards.

---

## Stage 5: Hotel check-in and first meal

### User goal
Stabilize after arrival and get through the first few local interactions.

### User behavior
- checks in
- looks for food
- considers first local trip or decides to rest

### User pain points
- low energy after travel
- communication friction
- food uncertainty
- too much pressure to optimize everything immediately

### Product opportunity
Recommend a low-risk first meal plan, basic hotel communication support, and a light first-day approach.

---

# 2. MVP task tree

The task tree below defines the prep logic that the product should use.

---

## A. Documents

### Main objective
Make essential identity and booking information accessible during travel.

### Subtasks
- check passport validity
- save visa or entry documents
- save flight confirmation
- save hotel confirmation
- create offline travel folder
- save passport copy
- keep printed backup if needed
- save itinerary summary

### MVP relevance
High

---

## B. Payments

### Main objective
Ensure the traveler can pay reliably and has a fallback if something fails.

### Subtasks
- choose main payment method
- prepare backup payment method
- test payment setup
- carry small emergency cash
- check card settings and limits
- keep payment troubleshooting note
- separate a backup payment option

### MVP relevance
Very high

---

## C. Internet

### Main objective
Make sure the traveler can get online or still function without immediate connectivity.

### Subtasks
- decide internet access plan
- set up connectivity before flying
- save key information offline
- test phone readiness
- save important addresses offline
- save key contacts offline
- prepare low-connectivity fallback plan

### MVP relevance
Very high

---

## D. Apps

### Main objective
Prepare the minimum digital toolset without overwhelming the traveler.

### Subtasks
- install core travel apps
- understand what each app is for
- keep the app list minimal
- log in before departure
- keep a short app cheat sheet
- prepare translation backup

### MVP relevance
Medium to high

---

## E. Transport

### Main objective
Reduce friction around arrival transfer and early local movement.

### Subtasks
- save hotel address in Chinese
- plan airport-to-hotel route
- save first-day local transport plan
- prepare city-to-city transition notes
- save Chinese names of key destinations
- save route screenshots
- allow extra time buffer

### MVP relevance
Very high

---

## F. Accommodation

### Main objective
Make hotel arrival and first-night logistics smooth.

### Subtasks
- save hotel name, address, and phone
- check arrival timing against check-in
- save booking number
- save hotel map screenshot
- prepare first-night comfort plan
- keep hotel check-in card ready

### MVP relevance
High

---

## G. Communication

### Main objective
Enable the traveler to survive basic real-world interactions with confidence.

### Subtasks
- prepare driver card
- prepare hotel check-in card
- prepare ask-for-help card
- learn a few useful phrases
- prepare a please-speak-slowly card
- prepare directions question card
- keep destination names ready to show

### MVP relevance
Very high for low-Chinese users

---

## H. Food and culture

### Main objective
Reduce food stress and basic cultural uncertainty.

### Subtasks
- prepare food restriction card
- plan first easy meal
- learn simple ordering method
- save a safe food communication card
- learn a few etiquette basics
- set low-pressure expectations for day one

### MVP relevance
High for users with restrictions, medium for general users

---

## I. Emergency and fallback

### Main objective
Prevent small failures from becoming high-stress problems.

### Subtasks
- prepare emergency fallback plan
- share itinerary with someone trusted
- prepare phone charging plan
- decide what to do if payment, internet, or transport fails

### MVP relevance
High

---

## J. Arrival day

### Main objective
Guide the traveler through the first few hours in a calm sequence.

### Subtasks
- review first 3 steps before landing
- confirm internet as early as possible
- open hotel info before leaving airport
- choose transport option before exiting
- keep destination address ready to show
- keep backup payment ready
- confirm essentials after check-in
- keep first meal simple
- do only one easy local trip if any
- review tomorrow before sleeping

### MVP relevance
Very high

---

# 3. Prioritization logic

Not every task should appear in every pack.

The system should classify tasks by:

- traveler profile
- first-time status
- Chinese ability
- travel party
- dietary or religious restrictions
- technology comfort
- number of cities
- main concerns

The output should usually show:

- 6-10 critical tasks
- 6-10 important tasks
- 3-6 optional tasks

This keeps the experience useful without overwhelming the user.

---

# 4. Key insight for product design

The highest-value part of the journey is not generic planning.
It is the operational and emotional gap between:

- knowing that China is interesting
and
- being truly ready to land and function confidently

That is where China Prep Pack should win.
